## About the Role

At Stanford AI Lab, I work at the intersection of systems engineering and machine learning research. My primary focus is making graph neural networks practical for real-world scale problems—graphs with billions of edges that don't fit in GPU memory.

## Key Projects

### GraphForge Framework

Led the development of a production-grade GNN training framework that enables researchers to work with massive graphs without worrying about infrastructure:

- **Hierarchical graph partitioning** for efficient distributed training
- **Custom CUDA kernels** for fused message passing operations
- **Automatic mixed precision** training with gradient checkpointing

### Knowledge Graph Reasoning

Collaborated with the NLP group on scaling reasoning over Wikidata (90M entities, 1.4B facts):

- Implemented efficient subgraph sampling strategies
- Developed novel attention mechanisms with O(n) complexity
- Achieved state-of-the-art results on OGBL-WikiKG2 benchmark

## Impact

The tools and research produced have been adopted by several industry partners including a major social media company for their recommendation systems.
